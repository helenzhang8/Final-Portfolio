Hotplate




For the "Iterative Design" project, I worked in a group to create a website for a startup. Without ever visiting the startup's website, we sketched out design ideas, made wireframes, and created hi-fi prototypes. We then finalized our prototypes after receiving feedback from an industry professional and conducted user testing.

We chose to design a website for the startup HotPlate.

The goal of Hotplate is to allow customers to order food from local chefs, as well as provide chefs with the platform and tools to sell their food. This startup aims to connect two needs: work for chefs out of business due to the pandemic and home-cooked meals for customers who are too busy to cook for themselves or would like to try something new.

Our web interface will be used by interested chefs, registered chefs, and customers looking to order food. With Hotplate, customers can order food safely from vetted home restaurants and support their community. Chefs are able to work at home and do what they love without the burdensome rental and maintenance fees involved with owning a restaurant. Hotplate’s platform handles ordering and provides cooks with analytics to help them grow.

Users are looking for an interesting alternative to the traditional restaurant experience. They are also looking for a trustworthy platform that emphasizes safety- Hotplate personally vets home restaurants, provides PPE, and encourages social distancing. The interface should attract users to try out this more community-based, albeit newer alternative.

Wireframes




View Wireframes

We wanted this website to appeal to both customers and chefs. It should be compelling to new users (both chefs and customers) and convince them to use the website. It should also be easy to use for returning users- customers should be able to order food and chefs should be able to manage their orders.

We chose to start off with a general Home page. This has a link to the Browse page for customers who would like to order food. Below is an introduction to the start up. Every page also has the Navigation bar, which leads to informational pages, as well as Sign In.

We chose to create a web interface as this would provide greater functionality, especially for chefs who use this to manage home-restaurants. We focused on the customer facing side for this project for the sake of testing. However, we also created a Chef Portal representative of the one a Hotplate chef would use.

Critique


  • Home Page
    • Even though "Browse home restaurants near you" is underlined, it is not interactive
  • FAQ Page
    • Too much information
    • Low color contrast (salmon and white) that can make it difficult for those who are visually impaired.
  • About Us Page
    • Missing button to submit "Contact Us" form
  • Browse Page
    • Search bar should not only be located at the bottom
    • Drop shadows are inconsistent and perhaps unnecessary when flat UI is more accessible
  • Restaurant Profile Page
    • Cart is not accessible - only visible from a specific restaurant's profile
    • Back button does not function

Testing


Task:

  • You are at home and you start to feel hungry.
  • YOUR TASK: Place an order for Wontons in Chili Oil from Michelle's Kitchen (do not log in).
  • IMPORTANT: This is an interactive prototype, not a real website. Just click to navigate.

Testing Instructions:

  1. Without leaving the homepage, describe what you would select first. Do not select anything yet. If you would leave without clicking anywhere, please explain why.
  2. Without leaving the homepage, what are your initial impressions of the website? Explain your answer.
  3. Navigate to the page where you can view food offerings available.
  4. Visit Michelle's Kitchen.
  5. Add Wontons in Chili Oil to your cart.
  6. Go to your cart.
  7. Without logging in or entering information, place your order. You will see an Order Confirmation screen.
  8. Any final thoughts? Please provide any final comments, questions, or suggestions you have before you stop recording and continue to the follow-up questions.

Post-Test Questions:

  1. What was the most difficult part of the task?
  2. How would you improve this site? The site layout/structure?
  3. What did you like about the site?
  4. Would you use this website? What changes would make you more likely to use this site?


Results




Post-Test Questions & Analysis:

  • User was slightly confused why the restaurant did not have a "menu" page and wasn't sure if the menu options displayed on the page were all that there was
  • User was thrown off by the fact that the prototype was not searchable - ie could not type to search in the initial browse page. Did not realize that just clicking search would take you to browsing.
  • User wanted to have more clarity on the delivery and pickup options
  • Multiple users did not realize the website could scroll. One tried to scroll using the scrollbar on the side that was just a placeholder.
  • User was confused about "Subject" option in checkout information page- this was a typo
  • Users generally felt the interface to be very familiar, and that the process was very streamlined. From adding to cart to order confirmation was always very quick.
  • The home-page has 3 different options to get to the browse page, and all 3 users picked a different one. More research would need to be done to discover if we should just streamline into one option, or if users like having different options.

Tasks:

  • Users had the most varying impressions/experiences with tasks 1-4. The Home page had a lot of options/methods to reach the browse page, and users took varying lengths of time to discover how to scroll on the page.
  • Task 5 varied in how fast it was done purely based on when the user discovered how to scroll on the prototype.
  • Tasks 6 and 7 were completed extremely efficiently, and all users reported that this felt streamlined and easy to navigate.

Takeaway:

  • It would be preferable to test a real website- users expected greater functionality
  • It would be interesting to better understand the possible options e.g. delivery and pick up, menu choices, to make the prototype more realistic
  • Perhaps we should refine options to get from the Home page to the Browse page
  • Maybe streamline information delivery and consider adjusting some of the page contents



Overall Takeaway


This assignment was an interesting opportunity to work on a group design project. The startup we chose was really interesting- I think it fulfills a need I've experienced many times during the pandemic, so I'm excited to see how things turn out.

Figma was a bit tedious to work with at times, but the collaborative environment it created was very helpful. The same applies to inVision, which allowed us to make wireframes together quite easily. This was especially helpful due to the remote nature of the project.

I'm also grateful that we had the opportunity to receive feedback from an expert in the field, as well as potential users. Everyone provided really valuable feedback, and I'm appreciative of the time they took to visit out website.

We sent our final prototype to Hotplate as the final part of our project. We hope that they enjoy seeing it, if not for the design, for the knowledge that we were inspired by their idea.
